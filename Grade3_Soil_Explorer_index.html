E1.1 â€“ Why Soil Is Important

Soil is very important for society and the environment. Plants grow in soil because it holds their roots, stores water, and provides nutrients. Many foods we eat such as fruits, vegetables, and grains come from plants that grow in soil. Soil is also home to living things like worms, insects, bacteria, and fungi. These living things break down dead plants and animals and return nutrients to the soil. Soil also filters water as it moves through the ground.

Humans can affect soil in good and bad ways. Pollution from chemicals, oil, and garbage can poison soil and make it unhealthy. Cutting down trees removes roots that hold soil in place, allowing it to wash away. Humans can help soil by composting, planting crops, and rotating crops. Roots help keep soil from washing away.
